Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by braising chicken in a sauce made up of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic and black pepper. It's tangy, salty, garlicy, and slightly sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 8 chicken drumsticks
  • 1 head of garlic (or less if you prefer)
  • ¾ cup soy sauce
  • ½ cup distilled white vinegar (or apple cider)
  • 1 tablespoon light or dark brown sugar
  • ½ tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1-2 bay leaves (optional, I will throw it in if I have it on hand)
  • 1-2 bay leaves (optional, I will throw it in if I have it on hand)
  • 1 cup uncooked rice

Steps:

  1. Place chicken, garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar and peppercorns in a large resealable plastic bag; toss to coat. Chill, turning chicken once, at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. Heat about 2 Tbsp of oil in a large saucepan or dutch oven at medium heat until shimmering.
  3. Add chicken in a single layer (you may need to do this in batches), reserve the marinade, and brown about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add reserved marinade, bay leaves if using, and enough water making sure to cover all pieces of meat and. Do not stir.
  5. Cover pan and when liquid begins to boil, turn down heat to simmer. Stir and simmer until chicken is tender and starting to pull away from the bone about 30-40 minutes.
  6. While the chicken is simmering, prepare rice according to the package description.
  7. Remove chicken and place on a clean plate. Turn up heat to medium-high and let reduce until sauce begins to glaze about 7-10 minutes. Return chicken to pot and coat with sauce.
  8. Serve chicken over rice and spoon sauce over.
